Product Overview

Flipper Zero is a portable multi-functional cybersecurity device designed for penetration testers, hardware hackers, and technology enthusiasts. Built around the powerful STM32WB55 dual-core microcontroller, it combines multiple wireless protocols, access control system interaction, and hardware debugging capabilities in a compact, autonomous device. Its playful cyber-dolphin companion evolves as you use the device, making learning and exploration engaging and fun.

Key Features

Sub-GHz Radio

Capture, analyze, and replay wireless signals from garage doors, IoT devices, and access control systems operating in 315-915 MHz range.

RFID & NFC

Read, write, and emulate both 125 kHz and 13.56 MHz cards including MIFARE, FeliCa, and other common access control formats.

Infrared Control

Universal remote functionality with built-in library for TVs, air conditioners, and other IR-controlled devices. Capture and replay IR signals.

BadUSB

Emulate USB HID devices like keyboards to execute DuckyScript payloads for penetration testing and automation tasks.

Hardware Hacking

GPIO pins with 3.3V logic (5V tolerant) for hardware debugging, firmware flashing, and interfacing with embedded systems.

Open Source

100% open-source hardware and firmware with active community development, custom applications, and extensible architecture.

iButton

Read, write, and emulate Dallas 1-Wire contact keys (TouchMemory) commonly used in access control and identification systems.

Autonomous

Completely standalone operation with 5-way directional pad, 1.4" LCD display, and 7-day standby battery life. No PC required.

Cyber-Dolphin Pet

Interactive Tamagotchi-like companion that evolves as you use the device, gamifying the learning experience and tracking progress.
